Enterprise Migration Playbook

A practical operating model for crypto inventory, CBOM, data lifetime, risk ranking, vendors, procurement, governance, rollout waves, and audit evidence.

CBOMInventoryRisk rankingGovernance

Educational reference only. Verify production decisions against current standards, vendor support, and security review.

1. Start with inventory

You cannot migrate what you cannot find. A useful crypto inventory records algorithms, certificates, libraries, keys, protocols, systems, owners, data lifetime, and vendor dependencies.

2. Classify risk

Urgency depends on data lifetime, exposure, criticality, migration difficulty, and regulatory pressure. Long-life confidential data and signing roots usually rank high.

Difficulty

3. Vendor and procurement readiness

Contracts and procurement should require crypto inventory, PQC roadmap, algorithm agility, support timelines, and evidence. Do not wait for renewal cycles to ask.

Decision matrix
RFP language

Ask for PQC support plans and supported standards.

SLA impact

Handshake size, latency, and device constraints may affect service metrics.

Support lifecycle

Long-lived devices need upgrade commitments.

Evidence

Attestations, validation, release notes, test results.

4. Governance and rollout

Migration needs a decision process: standards watch, exception review, architecture approval, security testing, rollback criteria, and operational monitoring.

Wave 1

Discovery, pilots, low-risk services.

Wave 2

External protocols and high-value data.

Wave 3

PKI, signing, hardware, and embedded systems.

Wave 4

Legacy closure, exceptions, and audit readiness.
