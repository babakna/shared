Use-Case Labs

Concepts stick when you apply them. Each lab is a realistic system. Read it, make the call, then compare with a worked recommendation. Use the same four questions every time.

  • Apply a repeatable decision framework to any system.
  • Choose key-exchange vs signature migrations appropriately.
  • Spot the ecosystem constraint that actually gates each rollout.

The four-question decision framework

Before any migration decision, walk these four questions. Every lab below is just this framework applied.

1

Lab 1 · Public website and API platform

A customer-facing web app plus a public REST API, behind a CDN and WAF, serving browsers and a 4-year-old mobile app.

Traffic is short-lived per session, but sessions carry auth tokens and some personal data. The mobile app pins certificates.

Where do you start, and what is the hidden landmine?

Recommended answer Start with hybrid ML-KEM key exchange at the edge (CDN/load balancer) — that is the harvest-now-decrypt-later protection. The landmine is the pinned mobile app and any middlebox that rejects larger handshakes: test the real client matrix and log the negotiated group. Certificate signatures stay classical until the PKI is ready. Job: key establishment; constraint: oldest client + pinning.

2

Lab 2 · Cloud service mesh and workload identity

Microservices authenticate to each other with short-lived mTLS certificates issued by an internal CA (SPIFFE-style), rotated hourly.

You fully control both ends and the issuing CA. Certificates live for an hour.

Is this easier or harder than the public web, and why?

Recommended answer Much easier — a rare "fast" case. You own every endpoint and the CA, clients update centrally, and hour-long certificates mean no long-lived signatures to worry about. You can move both key exchange and workload-identity signatures to PQC (or hybrid) quickly, with mesh metrics as your evidence. Internal, centrally-updated, short-lived is the ideal migration profile.

3

Lab 3 · Messaging and collaboration

An end-to-end encrypted chat product with frequently-updated mobile and desktop clients and a central directory.

Messages may be retained for years on devices; clients auto-update within weeks.

Which job matters most, and what makes this tractable despite long retention?

Recommended answer The key establishment in the key-agreement ratchet is the harvest-now-decrypt-later target — add PQC (hybrid) there so captured traffic stays safe. Long device retention matters, but fast, central client updates let you deploy the change to nearly everyone quickly. This is why modern messengers led PQC adoption.

4

Lab 4 · Data at rest and backups

A data lake and 7-year backups encrypted with AES-256, where data keys are wrapped by a KMS using RSA.

Someone panics: "AES is quantum-broken, we must re-encrypt petabytes!"

What is actually at risk, and what is the efficient fix?

Recommended answer AES-256 is fine (Grover only). The risk is the RSA key-wrapping: an attacker who harvests the wrapped keys could unwrap them post-quantum and then decrypt everything. Fix is cheap: move key wrapping to a PQC/hybrid KEM in the KMS and rewrap the data keys — no need to re-encrypt the petabytes of bulk data. Job: key establishment (wrapping), not bulk encryption.

5

Lab 5 · Firmware and device lifecycle

Industrial sensors with a 10-year field life verify firmware with an ECDSA signature checked by an immutable bootloader.

The bootloader's verification key and algorithm are burned in at manufacture and cannot be updated in the field.

What is the hardest problem here, and what must change now?

Recommended answer The hardest problem is the immutable verifier: you cannot teach existing bootloaders a new algorithm, so devices already in the field may be unfixable. What must change now is new hardware/bootloaders designed for agility (support PQC signatures, or dual verification) plus a plan for the installed base (compensating controls, planned replacement). Signatures + long life + immutable verifier is the worst-case combination — act earliest here.
